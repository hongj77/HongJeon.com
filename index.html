Hi, I'm Hong.

Full-stack web developer, Android developer, cheese enthusiast.

I study Computer Science and Mathematics at Cornell University..

Currently just finishing my first year, I have been living by the motto: learn, learn, and learn some more. Viewing every opportunity as a learning opportunity has helped my mind sprout in all directions and discipline. I always try to intake new information without any bias and without any filters. This philosophy has had a tremendous positive effect on the way I live life; I am always eager to learn something new. Teach me something!

Ultimately, I am interested in pursing a career path that involves machine learning, artificial intelligence, robotics, and entrepreneurship.

When I am not performing computer magic, I am a strong advocate for Starcraft 2, Eminem, dysfunctional pickup basketball games, barefoot ultimate frisbee, and the TV show Legend of Korra.

Big Red Hacks Site
Official website for the Cornell's student-run hackathon.

This is the my very first attempt at making a website. Code can be quite messy to read because some elements, such as the responsive navbar, were made from scratch. It was a tremedous learning experience that let me learn HTML and CSS in a short period of time.

A friend living on my floor co-founded the Big Red Hacks event. At the time, I was reading about making websites, so I immediately jumped at the opportunity to put my book-knowledge to application. I knew absolutely zero things about web development (sorry, Junia), and went on my merry way to work on this project. This was just the sort of head-first charge into unfamiliar territory I needed to jumpstart my HTML/CSS experience.
